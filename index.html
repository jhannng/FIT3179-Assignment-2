Annual Live Births and Deaths of Malaysia This visualization shows the number of live births and deaths in Malaysia, categorized by gender and ethnicity, providing insight into demographic trends.
Net Population Change Across State
In spite of its substantial growth, likely prompted by objects like urbanisation and economic opportunities; Selangor, the state with the darker red tone, has the greatest net population. Alternatively, states with paler red hues saw smaller net increases, indicating lower birth to death ratios or slower rates of growth.
Note: Hovering over an area provides the specifics of that state; and switch between the years 2012 and 2022 using the slider at the bottom to observe the manner in which the data evolves over time.
Comparision of Births and Deaths In States
Overall, there were more births than deaths in each state, most states have witnessed population expansion. But the difference between births and deaths varies by state; some have a larger difference, indicating faster growth, while others have a smaller difference, indicating slower demographic expansion.
Note: Hovering over the bar provides the specifics of that state; and switch between the years 2012 and 2022 using the slider at the bottom to observe the manner in which the data evolves over time.
Distribution of Births and Deaths by Gender In States
In overall, states like Selangor, have greater birth and death rates but states like Perlis and W.P. Labuan show lower numbers with darker colours. States vary somewhat in their male and female birth rates, but overall, they are balanced. One noteworthy observation is that states with higher birth rates tend to have higher death rates.
Note: Hovering over the bar provides the specifics of that combination; and switch between the years 2012 and 2022 using the slider at the bottom or filter by birth and death category and gender by dropdown menu to observe the manner in which the data evolves over time.
Ratios of Births and Deaths By Ethnic Group
In general, birth rates were greater than mortality rates in all ethnic groups, indicating potential increase in population. The Bumi Malay group is the one that has contributed the most to the population growth, followed by other groups with comparatively smaller numbers.
