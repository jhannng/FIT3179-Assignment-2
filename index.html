Net Population Change Across State
This visualization shows a map of Malaysia, with different states color-coded based on their net population change. The color scale ranges from light pink to dark red, indicating varying levels of population change across the country's regions.
Comparision of Births and Deaths In States
This chart displays the frequency of deaths (in blue) and live births (in green) for various states and territories in Malaysia. The data is presented as a horizontal bar graph, with each state's statistics shown side by side. Selangor appears to have the highest numbers for both categories, while smaller territories like W.P. Labuan and W.P. Putrajaya show the lowest frequencies. The graph allows for easy comparison of birth and death rates across different regions of Malaysia.
Distribution of Births and Deaths by Gender In States
This heatmap visualizes the distribution of births and deaths by sex across different states in Malaysia. It uses color intensity to represent the number of people, with darker shades indicating lower numbers and brighter colors (especially yellow) showing higher numbers. The chart is divided into four rows: death of males, death of females, birth of males, and birth of females. Selangor stands out with the brightest colors, suggesting it has the highest numbers across all categories. The visualization allows for easy comparison of demographic patterns across states and between genders.
Ratios of Births and Deaths By Ethnic Group
This chart compares birth and death rates across four ethnic groups in Malaysia: Bumi Malay, Bumi Other, Chinese, and Indian. The left panel shows birth rates, with Bumi Malay having significantly higher births than other groups. The right panel displays death rates, where Bumi Malay still leads but the gap is smaller. Chinese deaths notably exceed their birth rate, unlike other groups. This visualization effectively illustrates demographic differences among Malaysia's main ethnic communities.
